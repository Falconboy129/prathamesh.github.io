Overview

Conventional myoelectric prosthetic arms cost upward of $20,000, putting them out of reach for most people who need them. This project set out to prove that a functional, AI-assisted prosthetic forearm could be built for under $200 using off-the-shelf components and open-source tools.

How It Works

EMG Signal Capture

Surface EMG sensors placed on the forearm detect the electrical signals generated by muscle contractions. Raw signals are noisy and require significant preprocessing before they can be used for control.

AI Signal Smoothing

A neural network running on the Raspberry Pi filters and classifies the cleaned EMG signal in real time, mapping muscle activation patterns to specific finger gestures.

Raspberry Pi Processing

The Pi handles all neural network inference and acts as the central controller, passing gesture commands over serial to the ESP32 microcontroller.

ESP32 Servo Control

The ESP32 receives gesture commands and drives five servo motors — one per finger — via PWM signals, translating AI output into physical movement.

Mechanical Structure

The hand and finger linkages are fully 3D printed in PLA. Tendon-style fishing line runs through printed channels to translate servo rotation into finger curl.
